For Science? Brainstorm Document

In this document, we outline the proceedings of our brainstorm when coming up with our game concept, “For Science?”.

Where?

Our meeting took place in the upstairs portion of the WIT library. We chose this location for multiple reasons;

  • It has multiple round tables, ideal for meetings as everyone is facing each other

  • Moderate ambient noise is known to increase creativity*

When? How long?

The meeting took place on Wednesday 16th October 2019. The meeting went on for 2 hours, starting at 11 am and wrapping up at 1 pm.

Procedure:

Firstly we brainstormed the selected theme, “Labyrinth”. We brainstormed a few ideas based around the idea of labyrinths and mazes. We wrote all of our ideas down on post-it notes, categorizing them under “mechanic” and “aesthetic”.

As a creative aid, we decided to look at MDA structure and how it can be used to develop/influence the development of games. We looked on google for a few different images as inspiration when brainstorming the game’s aesthetic.

At the end of the meeting, we solidified the core idea for our game based on what we put down on our post-it notes.

* Mehta, Ravi, et al. “Is Noise Always Bad? Exploring the Effects of Ambient Noise On Creative Cognition.” Journal of Consumer Research, vol. 39, no. 4, 2012, pp. 784–799. JSTOR, www.jstor.org/stable/10.1086/665048.
