Who: 3 college students ranging in age from late teens to mid-20s. One participant was an avid board gamer while the other two had little experience with board games.

When: 12:20pm - 12:50pm on Tuesday the 26th of November

Where: LB18 in the library of the WIT cork road campus.

 

Reactions were favorable, although initially the participants struggled with grasping the rules. By the end of the game, however, they were playing by the rules.

During the playtest, they discussed any confusion they had with the rules among each other, allowing us to take notes on areas that caused difficulty with them.

Despite the initial confusion that arose during the start of the game, participants were able to reread the rules correctly, and in the remaining cases where the rules were unclear, the participants came to the intended interpretation of the rules through trial and error.

The below image is the feedback form we used to avoid leading questions.

We decided that a sheet to fill in would be more likely to yield unbiased feedback than having to give the feedback directly to us.

Clarity was what the game scored (4-5) lowest on with testers saying that while the rules were generally clear there were sections that were difficult to understand.

For Flow the game scored (6-7) quite highly with testers saying that since turns were quick they didn't feel bored waiting for their turns.

For Balance the game again scored (6-7) quite highly as according to the testers they all felt they had a good chance of winning through out the game.

For Duration, the game once again scored (5-6). High, but this had the widest range of ratings receiving a 4, 6, and 7.

Integration was one of the aspects where the game scored (5-6) lower with two 5s and a 6.

Fun was the highest rated (6-7) aspect of the game receiving two 7s and a 6.

Interaction scored (6) a 5, 6, and 7.
